Counselling

I offer integrative therapy which combines different counselling models and skills to help clients face and understand their presenting problems. Using an integrative model enables you to explore problems emanating from affective, cognitive, behavioural and physiological problem areas. This can be explained as feelings/ emotional, thoughts, actions, biological respectively. Integrative therapy helps to fill in developmental gaps that affect your development in many ways.

The integrative model is a holistic in a sense that is flexible and helpful in tackling a wide variety of difficulties and incorporated into many different kinds of presenting problems such as working with adults, children, couples. This model is suitable for managing low self-esteem issues, relationship difficulties, depression, anxiety, personality disorders, eating disorders and many many more. Your role is an active one whereby you tell me about your problems and I will judge which evidence-based approach is suitable for you based on your difficulties, your personality type, your needs physical attributes and beliefs.

CBT is about how you think about yourself, the world and other people. How what you do affects your thoughts and feelings. Person Centred Counselling is an assessment of need that puts you at the centre of the process and is based on your views, choices and preferences.

CBT (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy) CBT can help you to change how you think ("Cognitive") and what you do ("Behaviour") to make you think better and feel better. Instead of focussing on the causes of your distress or symptoms in the past, it looks for ways to improve your state of mind NOW. CBT has been found to be helpful in Anxiety, Depression, Panic, Agoraphobia, Social phobia, Bulimia, Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD), post-traumatic stress disorder and Schizophrenia. CBT can help you to break vicious circle. CBT can help make you feel good, better and think well. CBT can help you to learn things to help you cope with your problems yourself.

How do I work?

I will help you break each problem down into its separate parts and may ask you to keep a diary to identify your individual patterns of thoughts, emotions, bodily feelings and actions.

Together we will look at your thoughts, feelings and behaviours to work out:

- if they are unrealistic or unhelpful. - how they affect each other, and you.

  • I will then help you to work out how to change unhelpful thoughts and behaviours
  • Home work will be recommended if you wish to practise changes in your everyday life.
  • Depending on the situation, you might start to:
    -Question a self-critical or upsetting thought and replace it with a positive (and more realistic) one that you have developed in CBT.
    -Recognise that you are about to do something that will make you feel worse and, instead, do something more helpful.
    -At each meeting you discuss how you've got on since the last session.
    -I will help with suggestions if any of the tasks seem too hard or don't seem to be helping.
  • I will not ask you to do things you don't want to do - you decide the pace of the treatment and what you will and won't try.

The strength of CBT is that you can continue to practise and develop your skills even after the sessions have finished. This makes it less likely that your symptoms or problems will return. This atmosphere will enable you to understand who you are and how the events of your life have shaped your relationship with yourself, others and the world. The sessions are for those who are finding it difficult at work and at home in any area of life e.g. marriage, school, parenting, work, and relationship. Psychodynamic Therapy

This therapy draws on childhood and early experiences including unconscious conflicts to help understand your present difficulty emotions. Psychodynamic therapy explores and identifies patterns of behaviours and interactions that are deemed unhelpful. This therapy will also help people with depression identify and address problems in their relationships with family, partners and friends. The idea is that poor relationships with people in your life can leave you feeling depressed which will in turn. make your relationships with other people worse leading you to feel more depressed.

  • Psychodynamic therapy emphasises the significance of unconscious conflicts and their relation to development
  • It Identifies defences as developing in internal psychic structures in order to avoid unpleasant consequences of conflict
  • It believes that psychopathology develops especially from early childhood experiences
  • It is also of the view that internal representations of experiences are organized around interpersonal relations
  • It believes that life issues and dynamics will re-emerge in the context of the client-therapist relationship as transference and counter-transference
  • It uses of free association as a major method for exploration of internal conflicts and problems
  • The focus on interpretations of transference, defences mechanisms, and current symptoms and the working through of these present problems
  • The therapy views insight as critically important for success in therapy

This therapy views the relationship between the therapist and the client is vital to understand and work through the relationship difficulties and emotional difficulties. Psychodynamic therapy is useful in managing deep seated and rooted disorders and emotional difficulties, when someone seems to have lost life's meaning, difficulty forming and maintaining lasting personal relationships. This therapy can also help with addictions, eating disorders and some social anxiety disorders. You will be encouraged to speak freely about current issues, fears, dreams, fantasies, desires that hold you back or keep you stuck and powerless. The aim is to increase self-esteem, develop a sense of self, increased coping, resilience and improved and satisfying relationships

Person Centred Counselling

You know yourself better, I do not profess to know you more than you know yourself. What I will do is to make every attempt to foster an environment in which you can encounter ‘yourself’ and become more intimate with your own thoughts, feelings and meanings. The idea behind this approach is that psychological disturbance occurs when your ‘self-concept’ begins to clash with immediate personal experience such as when the evidence of what you feel or do clashes with what you think you ‘ought’ to be feeling are doing. This sometimes happen when your positive view of yourself depends on the positive judgments of others. In other words, you feel worthy only when others are happy or satisfied with you. So as long as rely on a self-concept designed that meets the needs of others as opposed to yourself you will find that you are unhappy in many ways. This approach maintains that three core conditions provide a climate conducive to growth and therapeutic change.

  • Unconditional positive regard
  • Empathic understanding
  • Congruence

This means that your will be able to explore all thoughts and feelings, positive or negative, without danger of rejection or condemnation. You will have the freedom to explore and to express yourself in any way you wish without having to meet any particular ‘acceptable’ standards and I will be genuinely interested in what you have to say to become the person that you really are and not others want you to be!

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT) draws on mindfulness and behaviour activations (BA) to encourage clients to increase the ability to engage in value-based, positive behaviours while experiencing difficult thoughts, emotions or sensations. According to ACT, psychological problems and distressed are caused by avoidance and an inability or difficulty in detaching from painful experiences. Fixating on these painful experiences which leads to a failure to take much needed action in suitable to your core values.

According to ACT, many psychological problems are underpinned by FEAR:

  • Fusion with your thoughts on painful events
  • Excessive evaluation of experience
  • Avoidance of repeat experience
  • Reason-giving for your unhelpful behaviours
  • Accept your past thoughts, feelings and behaviours and be present
  • Choose a valued direction
  • Take action

    • These steps will lead to take positive action, reduce unhelpful thoughts, feelings and behaviours. Reduce distressing psychological symptoms, increase coping and reduced stress. This is because ACT focusses on 6 core processes: Firstly

  • Cognitive diffusion ACT enables you to observe uncomfortable thoughts, images, emotions and memories and gently and slowly detaching any value laden interpretations to them and becoming one with the
  • Acceptance: This allows your thoughts to come and go without struggling with them
  • Contact with the present moment: This allows you focus your awareness on the here and now and to direct your attention flexibly and voluntarily to present external and internal events rather than automatically focussing on the past or future
  • Observing the self: This allows you to have become one with yourself be conscious of your emotions as ‘ongoing’ and not static
  • Values: Discovering what is most important to you
  • Committed action: This allows you to set goals according to your personal values and taking action.
